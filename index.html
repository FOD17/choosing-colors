Choosing Colors for Your Websites

Choose and tweak color palettes. And fix when you choose colors and nothing seems to work together.

Color

The general term used to describe hue, tint, tone, shade, and temperature.

Hue

Refers to the dominant Color Family of the specific color we're looking at.

White, Black, and Grey are never referred to as a Hue

Tint

Hue or mixture of pure colors with only White added (or moving closer to white)

Doesn't make it brighter, but lightens the color

Tone

Hue or mixture of pure colors with only Gray added (or moving closer to gray)

This tones down the intensity of a color

Shade

Hue or mixture of pure colors with only Black added (or moving closer to black)

No white or gray

Temperature

Our perception of color as being either warm or cool

Warm: Add red or yellow to a hue

Cool: Add blue or green to a hue

Color Models

CMYK: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black.

This uses "subtractive" mixing of light to derive different colors.

Subtractive means to cover up the whiteness on the page


RGB: Red, Blue, Green.

This uses "additive" mixing of light to derive different colors.

Like having 3 bright red, green, and blue lights projecting at you.


HSB (V): Hue, Saturation, and Brightness.

This uses a mixture of these attributes to derive different colors.

Changes the purity and density of color as well as how much light is projected through the color.

Very common.

How he see color and how our brains work.
